Web accessibility is a crucial aspect of online platform design and development that is often underestimated. It refers to how easy it is for people with different abilities and limitations to access, understand and use a website. At its core, it is about ensuring that all users, regardless of their individual needs and requirements, have the same opportunity to access and navigate digital content. Web accessibility not only affects people with disabilities, but also older people, people with temporary disabilities (such as injuries) and users with slower internet connections or outdated technological devices. By removing barriers and improving accessibility, we are creating a more inclusive online environment that has the potential to enrich and improve the lives of millions of people.
In this article, we'll take a closer look at what web accessibility means, why it's important and how it can be achieved to ensure an inclusive and equal online experience for all. This is the accessibility statement from Projekt City Ltd.!
#1 Measures to support accessibility
Projekt City Ltd. takes the following measures to ensure accessibility of Projekt City:
  • Assign clear accessibility goals and responsibilities
  • A simple and easy to understand version of our main page: https://itsmarian.wixsite.com/projektcity / https://projektcity.github.io/
  • Include accessibility throughout our internal policies
  • Using a uniform system for all web pages and simple colors, font families and font sizes
#2 Conformance status
The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA. Projekt City is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.

Neccessarry Cookies
Cookies are small text files that are sent to the user's browser, desktop client or device when they visit the website. These are automatically provided by the device when the page is visited again. Cookies are mostly used by websites to improve the user experience, increase the performance of the site and - if activated - display personalized content.
The law states that companies may automatically store necessary cookies on devices without user consent if they are necessary for the operation of the site.
For all other types of cookies, such as analytics and statistics, preferences and personalization, marketing, advertising and all other cookies, the user's consent is required.
You can change your cookie settings at any time. To do this, go to the bottom of one of our pages, click on "change cookie preferences" and select your preferences.
Statistics and Analytics
Statistics and analysis cookies, also known as "performance cookies," help the website to track how users interact with the site, which link is clicked by them, how long they visit it, which pages are particularly popular and which pages the users come from. This information is collected anonymously and is not trackable and helps us to better understand our site and users and their behavior in order to provide a better experience.
Preferences and Personalization
Preference cookies, are cookies that allow the website to remember your choices you made through your use of our website, such as language and regional preferences, or what your username and password are so that they can be retrieved automatically the next time you load the page, leading to a overall better performance of the webpage.
Marketing and Advertisement
Marketing cookies are mainly used for advertising purposes by us and our third parties.
